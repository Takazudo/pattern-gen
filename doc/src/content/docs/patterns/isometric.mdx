# Isometric

Grid of isometric cubes with three visible faces shaded at different brightness levels, creating an Escher-like 3D impossible geometry illusion.

## Algorithm

The pattern draws a field of isometric cubes in a hexagonal grid arrangement:

1. **Hexagonal grid layout**: Cubes are placed on a staggered grid where even rows are aligned and odd rows are offset by half the horizontal spacing. The horizontal spacing is `cubeSize × √3` and the vertical spacing is `cubeSize × 1.5`. This hexagonal arrangement ensures cubes tessellate perfectly in isometric projection.

2. **Three-face cube drawing**: Each cube is drawn as three parallelogram faces visible in isometric projection:
   - **Top face** (diamond shape): Drawn as the lightest face, brightened from the base color by the Top Brightness parameter. Vertices form a horizontal diamond above the cube center.
   - **Left face**: Drawn in the base color at its original brightness. Extends from the center downward and to the left.
   - **Right face**: Drawn as the darkest face, darkened by the Right Darkness parameter. Extends from the center downward and to the right.

3. **Color assignment**: Each cube position is pre-assigned a random color from the palette (rotated by a seed-based offset for variety). The three faces then derive their brightness from this single base color, maintaining color coherence per cube.

4. **Edge definition**: All three faces receive thin dark outline strokes along their edges, providing crisp definition between adjacent cubes.

5. **Back-to-front rendering**: Cubes are drawn top to bottom (row by row) so that lower cubes correctly overlap upper ones, maintaining the illusion of depth.

## Parameters

| Parameter | Range | Default | Description |
|-----------|-------|---------|-------------|
| Grid Divisions | 6–30 | 14 | Number of cubes across the longest dimension. Controls cube size. |
| Top Brightness | 0–0.5 | 0.3 | How much to lighten the top face. Higher values increase the "lit from above" effect. |
| Right Darkness | 0.2–0.9 | 0.65 | How much to darken the right face. Higher values increase shadow contrast. |

## Visual Characteristics

The isometric pattern creates a striking optical illusion of three-dimensional cubes stacked in impossible space — reminiscent of M.C. Escher's geometric artwork. The brain perceives the flat drawing as either a field of cubes seen from above or as concave hollows seen from below, and the interpretation can flip as you look at it. At low Grid Divisions, the cubes are large and the 3D illusion is dramatic. At high divisions, the cubes become small and the pattern reads more as a textured surface. The Top Brightness and Right Darkness parameters control the strength of the 3D illusion — more contrast between faces makes the cubes appear more volumetric, while reduced contrast flattens them toward a hexagonal tiling. With a diverse color palette, the pattern becomes a colorful mosaic of cubes; with a monochromatic scheme, the geometric structure dominates.
