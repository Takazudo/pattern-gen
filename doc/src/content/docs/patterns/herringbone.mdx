# Herringbone

Rectangular bricks arranged in a V-shaped zigzag layout, recreating the classic parquet flooring pattern with alternating horizontal and vertical bricks.

## Algorithm

The pattern constructs a herringbone layout by alternating horizontal and vertical rectangular bricks in a staggered arrangement:

1. **Brick dimensions**: Each brick has a 3:1 width-to-height ratio. The brick width is determined by dividing the canvas by Grid Divisions and adjusting for zoom. This ratio is key to the herringbone geometry — the short side of one brick aligns with the long side of the next.

2. **V-unit construction**: The repeating unit consists of four bricks arranged in two rows. In the first row, a horizontal brick is placed followed by a vertical brick to its right. In the second row, the same pair is shifted rightward by one brick height, creating the characteristic V-shaped zigzag. The horizontal unit width is `brickW + brickH` and the vertical unit height is `2 × brickH`.

3. **Color variation**: Colors are pre-generated for determinism. Each brick picks a random base color from the palette and applies a random brightness variation (lighter or darker by up to the Color Variation amount). This simulates the natural variation found in real wood or brick materials.

4. **Gap rendering**: A configurable gap between bricks (controlled by Gap Factor) simulates mortar or spacing. Each brick is inset from its full cell by the gap amount on all sides.

5. **Edge shading**: Each brick has a lighter top or left edge highlight and a darker bottom or right edge shadow, drawn as thin strokes. This gives each brick a subtle beveled appearance suggesting depth.

## Parameters

| Parameter | Range | Default | Description |
|-----------|-------|---------|-------------|
| Grid Divisions | 5–20 | 10 | Number of bricks across the longest dimension. Controls overall brick size. |
| Gap Factor | 0–0.2 | 0.08 | Gap between bricks as a fraction of brick height. Simulates mortar or spacing. |
| Color Variation | 0–0.3 | 0.15 | Maximum brightness variation per brick. Higher values create more natural, varied surfaces. |

## Visual Characteristics

The herringbone pattern is immediately recognizable as a classic flooring or brickwork layout. The V-shaped zigzag creates strong directional movement across the surface. At low Grid Divisions, the bricks are large and the geometric structure is bold and architectural. At high divisions, the pattern becomes a fine, fabric-like texture. The Gap Factor has a significant visual impact — at 0 the bricks are seamless and the pattern reads as solid geometry, while higher values introduce visible "grout lines" that emphasize individual bricks. Color Variation controls the liveliness of the surface — at 0 each palette color appears flat and uniform, while at maximum the surface has the rich, organic quality of natural materials where no two pieces are exactly alike.
